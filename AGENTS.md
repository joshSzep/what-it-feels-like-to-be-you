# AGENTS.md

This repository is for planning, outlining, and drafting the novel *What It Feels Like to Be You*.

This file is the authoritative project guide for any agent working in this repo. Preserve canon, tone, and thematic direction unless the user explicitly changes them.

## 1. Project Purpose

- **Title:** *What It Feels Like to Be You*
- **Form:** hard science-fiction literary philosophical novel / novella
- **Target structure:** 18 chapters
- **Setting:** approximately 15 years in the future
- **Primary geography:** Los Angeles, with select scenes in secure clinical and political settings
- **World model:** AI and robotics have advanced, but everyday life is still recognizably human and contemporary; there is no total social transformation

## 2. Non-Negotiables

- The book must remain **positive, inspiring, and uplifting**.
- The story may pass through grief, addiction, abuse, scandal, and political conflict, but it must not become nihilistic.
- The novel's deepest wonder is **relational**, not technological.
- Empathy is powerful, but it is not magic and it is not morally self-justifying.
- Technology cannot replace the slow human work of love, responsibility, boundaries, or care.
- Matt does **not** end up with Tifa romantically.
- Matt's ending is a blend of:
  - self-forgiveness
  - ethical purpose
  - chosen family
- By the end, Matt becomes effectively **Uncle Matt** to Piyush's children.

## 3. Core Premise

Matt, a brilliant but emotionally unmoored AI researcher and neuroscientist, co-creates a machine that allows one person to directly experience the raw emotions of another. At the deepest level, he is driven by the belief that if he had truly understood what his first love felt twenty years earlier, he would have made different life choices and not lost her. But once the machine leaves the lab, it becomes at once a therapeutic breakthrough, a diplomatic sensation, an addictive escape, and a threat to the last refuge of privacy: the human interior.

The novel asks whether empathy can deepen human connection without becoming a new instrument of power, escape, coercion, or commerce.

## 4. Thematic Spine

### Central thematic statement
Connection matters. Empathy enables connection. Compassion for another person's suffering can relieve one's own suffering.

### Core beliefs
- Human beings are less alone than they think.
- Much cruelty grows out of pain, ignorance, fear, and self-protection.
- Understanding does not erase accountability.
- Real intimacy requires responsibility, not just access.
- Healing is possible without perfect repair.
- A hopeful ending does not require wish fulfillment.

### Final emotional destination
The final movement of the book should land in **love and acceptance**.

## 5. Tone and Style

- **Mode:** literary, philosophical, hard-SF
- **Emotional texture:** intimate, restrained, reflective, precise
- **Scientific texture:** plausible and bounded; the speculative leap should feel clean and rigorous
- **Touchstone:** emotionally restrained literary speculative fiction in the neighborhood of *Never Let Me Go*, but not derivative
- **Writing rule:** when choosing between melodrama and restraint, choose restraint

## 6. POV and Time Structure

- Use **rotating POVs**.
- **Matt opens chapter one** and remains the emotional center.
- Important POV carriers include:
  - Matt
  - Piyush
  - Shelby
  - Marcus
  - Farah
  - Arjun
  - Tifa (later; partly withheld at first)
- Do **not** use formal flashback chapters for Matt and Tifa.
- Reveal the past through:
  - memory
  - conversation
  - emotional realization

## 7. The Future World

- The story is set about 15 years from now.
- AI and robotics have advanced in familiar ways.
- Society remains recognizably contemporary.
- The speculative center of the novel is not the whole world; it is the breakthrough itself.

## 8. The Invention

### Name
**The Empathy Engine**

### Company
**Qualia Labs**

### What it does
The Empathy Engine transfers **raw emotional state** from one person to another.

It does **not** transfer:
- thoughts
- explicit memories
- full narrative content
- full sensory scenes

It does transfer:
- raw feeling state
- embodied affective load
- dynamic emotional patterning

### Scientific model
Use a plausible hard-SF explanation:
- noninvasive full headsets
- clinical chairs with dozens of sensors
- neural and interoceptive data capture
- AI-derived person-independent affect mapping
- stimulation of corresponding emotional-state patterns in the recipient

Recorded sessions can later be replayed on stripped-down illicit receiver rigs that use only the stimulation side of the system, bypassing capture hardware and clinical safeguards.

### Subjective feel
The onset feels **like déjà vu**.

### Key limitation
Users struggle to distinguish between:
- their own emotions
- the linked emotions coming from the other person

### Major dangers
1. Emotional addiction
2. Violation despite consent
3. Emotional privacy breach
4. Commercialization of inner life
5. Coercive institutional use

### End-state of the technology
By the end of the novel, the Empathy Engine survives, but only as a tightly restricted tool for:
- therapy
- diplomacy / conflict resolution
- tightly controlled clinical use

Congress classifies it as a dangerous, tightly regulated intervention comparable in legal seriousness to a controlled therapeutic drug.

## 9. Canonical Structural Spine

### Story duration
About **one year** from the opening dinner to the final scene.

### Opening image
Matt eating at a high-end steakhouse in Los Angeles with Piyush and Shelby after the first successful trial run earlier that day.

### Ending image
Matt with Piyush at Griffith Park watching Piyush's children play.

### Major turns
- **Inciting movement:** the first successful human trial proves the machine works
- **Early escalation:** Shelby starts pushing the technology beyond the lab
- **Act I break:** Shelby publicly pushes the machine into the world after the diplomats' success
- **Midpoint:** a user becomes addicted to recorded emotional sessions and dies by suicide after access is cut off
- **Second-half escalation:** emotional sessions can be captured, replayed, leaked, and weaponized
- **Darkest point:** Matt believes he has helped create a system for commercializing the human soul
- **Climax:** Matt tells Congress the truth and sacrifices the machine's commercial future
- **Resolution:** the machine is restricted; Shelby changes; Matt finds peace, purpose, and chosen family

## 10. Character Canon

### Matt
- **Age:** 44
- **Profession:** AI researcher and neuroscientist
- **Role:** co-creator of the Empathy Engine
- **Present life:** brilliant, unstable, single, childless, worldly, inwardly lonely
- **Core wound:** he failed his first love, Tifa, by choosing his own need to explore life over a stable shared future
- **Driving belief:** if he had understood Tifa, he would have chosen differently
- **Hidden drive behind the invention:** he wants to solve the distance between selves; at bottom, he wants to understand Tifa
- **Opening emotional state:** pride mixed with dread
- **False equation:** intensity = depth
- **Arc:** from unrooted brilliance and guilt toward responsibility, self-forgiveness, and belonging

### Piyush
- **Age:** 39
- **Role:** Matt's brilliant colleague and co-creator
- **Outer life:** stable, married, father of two
- **Marriage:** to Lina
- **Children:** Mira (10), Nikhil (6)
- **Mutual envy with Matt:** Matt envies Piyush's rooted family life; Piyush envies Matt's breadth of experience and stories
- **Private fear:** success will devour the family life he most values
- **Arc:** becomes Matt's brother-like moral anchor

### Lina
- **Role:** Piyush's wife
- **Initial dynamic with Matt:** friendly but distant
- **Shift:** after using the machine with Piyush, she understands how much Piyush respects Matt
- **End-state:** she begins to see Matt as family

### Mira
- **Age:** 10
- **Function:** part of the family world Matt gradually joins

### Nikhil
- **Age:** 6
- **Function:** writes a song about tying your shoe
- **Story function:** helps reveal to Matt the beauty of ordinary love and presence

### Shelby
- **Role:** angel investor behind Qualia Labs
- **Outer drive:** scale, influence, money, power, impact
- **Inner contradiction:** she genuinely believes the machine could help humanity, but she also wants to win
- **Private wound:** estranged from her father, a veteran with PTSD and alcoholism who abused Shelby and her mother
- **Stated desire:** wants him to understand the pain he caused
- **Secret desire:** wants to understand the pain that made him what he became
- **Arc:** from commercialization and control toward humility, pity, reconciliation, and relinquishment
- **End-state:** gives up ownership / control so the Empathy Engine cannot become a mass-market product

### Tifa
- **Role:** Matt's first love
- **History:** no contact with Matt for 20 years at the start
- **Past misunderstanding:** Matt believed he failed her by refusing stability; in truth, she herself was not ready for marriage
- **Why she couldn't say that then:** she was following the default life plan
- **What she really wanted then:** to go back to school
- **What she studied:** family medicine
- **Present life:** family physician, married, mother of three
- **Marriage:** fundamentally happy and stable
- **Husband:** Shu, a firefighter, solid and dependable
- **Function in Matt's arc:** allows him to learn he did not ruin her life and helps release him from false guilt
- **Important:** she is not the romantic endgame; she is the source of closure, truth, and enduring care

### Shu
- **Role:** Tifa's husband
- **Profession:** firefighter
- **Character note:** good, solid, emotionally credible
- **Canon note:** he knows about Matt and Tifa's history

### Marcus
- **Role:** the waiter from the opening dinner; later the novel's addiction tragedy
- **Present life:** service worker in Los Angeles trying to stay sober
- **Substance history:** addicted to ketamine
- **Core deprivation:** came from a poor and broken home
- **Sexuality:** closeted gay man
- **Reason he remains closeted:** his father was homophobic, and Marcus has spent his life trying to be the macho son his father wanted
- **Relationship to the machine:** receives an anonymous email linking to illicit recorded emotional sessions and a stripped-down black-market playback rig
- **Function:** shows how borrowed feeling can become the ultimate drug when detached from real relationship

### Rachel
- **Role:** Matt's personal friend in Los Angeles
- **Profession:** film director
- **Why she uses the machine:** to accelerate trauma therapy
- **Buried trauma:** childhood sexual abuse
- **Crisis role:** her therapy breakdown is leaked and turned into scandal
- **After the leak:** she fights back publicly and is mobbed by trolls and haters

### Farah Qureshi
- **Role:** Pakistani diplomat
- **Public posture:** disciplined hardliner
- **Private wound:** her son died in a border conflict
- **Pre-session desire:** she wants Arjun to understand the pain his country has caused

### Arjun Mehta
- **Role:** Indian diplomat
- **Public posture:** self-controlled hardliner
- **Private wound:** his daughter died in the same border conflict
- **Pre-session desire:** he wants Farah to understand the pain his country has suffered too

### Shelby's father
- **Role:** estranged father; veteran with PTSD; alcoholic; formerly abusive
- **Function:** catalyst for Shelby's transformation
- **In the machine:** Shelby first feels his shame; he first feels her fear

## 12. Canonical Scenes and Beats

### Opening dinner
- Los Angeles steakhouse
- Matt, Piyush, Shelby celebrate the first successful trial
- Matt feels pride and dread
- Shelby leaves an absurdly generous tip
- she remarks, in essence, that people will pay far more than that to feel understood
- Matt realizes the machine will not stay contained

### Piyush breakfast scene
- next morning at home
- family warmth and domestic rhythm
- Nikhil's shoe-tying song
- Shelby appears on a morning show and invades the domestic space with publicity

### Shelby morning-show sequence
- opens in the makeup chair
- she sees a birthday message from her father: “Happy Birthday”
- she feels rage and swallows it
- on air, she promises a clinical rollout within a year and frames the machine as a tool for therapy, diplomacy, and repairing damaged relationships

### Marcus introduction
- Marcus watches Shelby on television before the restaurant opens
- his vulnerable internal response is: “I want this.”
- later he receives an anonymous email with the subject line: “What It Feels Like To Be You”

### Diplomats' session
- use real nations: India and Pakistan
- POV split between Farah and Arjun
- they first receive each other's grief
- then each other's compassion for that grief
- the key line is: “I thought only we buried children.”
- public result: an embrace, not a grand speech

### Tifa re-entry
- happens in early Act II, a few days after Matt becomes publicly famous from the diplomats' session
- first text from Tifa: “You look good for 44”
- Matt replies: “I spend a lot of time in the jimjilbangs”
- first in-person meeting: a quiet Korean café in Koreatown
- emotional surprise: the meeting is easy, not awkward

### Rachel leak sequence
- Rachel uses the machine in trauma therapy
- her breakdown is captured / leaked by HumansRHumans
- public cruelty follows
- this makes the privacy threat intimate and personal for Matt

### Piyush / Matt restorative sequence
- Piyush and Matt use the machine together
- Matt experiences Piyush's pride as Nikhil plays the shoe-tying song
- Matt realizes he has mistaken intensity for depth
- ordinary rooted love becomes newly visible to him as one of life's deepest experiences

### Matt / Tifa session
- happens before the Congressional testimony
- first transmitted emotion: love
- Matt finally understands Tifa was not ready for marriage either
- Tifa finally understands Matt was not merely selfish; he was frightened, trapped in a false life script, and unable to articulate it
- Matt leaves carrying grief, love, clarity, and quiet steadiness

### Congressional testimony
- Matt is summoned to speak
- he is honest
- the hardest truth he says is that he is afraid of the machine
- he sacrifices the commercial future of his own invention
- result: the machine is tightly restricted to therapeutic and diplomatic use

### Shelby / father reconciliation
- happens after Congress
- Shelby feels his shame
- he feels her fear
- he tries to flee the feeling, then stays
- he cries, recognizes what he did, and apologizes
- Shelby reconciles with him

### Shelby's final act
- she gives up ownership / control so the machine cannot become a mass-market product

### Final scene
- Griffith Park playground
- Matt stands with Piyush watching the kids play
- emotional note: not spectacle, but belonging

## 13. Antagonistic Forces

There is no single cartoon villain.

The antagonistic pressures are:
- ambition without restraint
- markets that commercialize intimacy
- institutions that want leverage
- hidden actors exploiting real vulnerabilities
- the human temptation to use feeling as a shortcut around responsibility

### HumansRHumans
- hacker group targeting brain-computer interface projects
- believes it is protecting the last refuge of privacy: the human mind
- deliberately tries to create public tragedy to discredit the technology
- remains hidden rather than becoming a public-facing movement
- is morally **uncomfortably both right and wrong**

## 14. Ethical Position of the Novel

Avoid both naive techno-utopianism and total techno-dread.

The novel's position is:
- empathy can be transformative
- inner life must not become a casual commodity
- consent is not enough unless people understand the vulnerability involved
- not all human pain should be transferable, replayable, or sellable
- some forms of understanding should remain slow, bounded, and protected

## 15. Character Arc Summaries

### Matt
Matt begins as a man trying to invent his way back across the distance that cost him love, and ends as a man who accepts that he cannot repair the past, only become more capable of loving rightly in the present.

### Shelby
Shelby begins as a woman determined to scale empathy into the world, and ends as a woman who learns that some breakthroughs remain humane only if they are bounded, costly, and protected from appetite.

### Piyush
Piyush begins as the stable man Matt envies and quietly envies Matt in return, and ends as the friend who helps Matt understand that the truest form of greatness may be ordinary commitment, presence, and care.

### Tifa
Tifa begins as the lost figure haunting Matt's life and becomes the person whose truth releases him from the false story that he destroyed her, allowing both of them to reframe their past with love instead of regret alone.

## 17. Working Rules for Future Agents

- Preserve continuity. Do not casually rename characters, alter arcs, or change the thematic destination.
- If the user asks for new scenes, outlines, or chapters, extend from this canon rather than starting over.
- If a proposed idea conflicts with the uplifting moral horizon of the project, revise it before presenting it.
- Keep the machine bounded: it transfers emotion, not thoughts or complete memories.
- Keep Tifa and Matt's past revealed through present-tense interaction, memory, and conversation rather than dedicated flashback chapters.
- When writing high-emotion scenes, prefer specificity and restraint over speeches and melodrama.
- If creating planning documents, place them under the `notes/` directory unless the user asks for a different structure.
- If uncertain, favor choices that deepen compassion, responsibility, and ordinary human intimacy.

## 18. Priority Order When Making Decisions

When tradeoffs arise, prioritize in this order:

1. Preserve the book's uplifting emotional truth
2. Preserve character continuity
3. Preserve hard-SF credibility and bounded rules
4. Preserve literary restraint
5. Preserve plot momentum
