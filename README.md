# What It Feels Like to Be You

![What It Feels Like to Be You cover](cover.png)

## Blurb

What if empathy stopped being metaphor and became technology?

Fifteen years in the future, neuroscientist Matt and his colleague Piyush help create the Empathy Engine, a machine that allows one person to directly experience the raw emotions of another. At first, it looks like a miracle: therapists can reach patients in ways language never allowed, estranged families glimpse one another across old distances, and even bitter political enemies discover that grief does not respect borders.

But Matt did not build the machine for the world alone. Beneath the science is an older wound: twenty years ago, he lost the first woman he ever loved, and some part of him has never stopped believing that if he had truly understood what she felt, he would have chosen differently.

As the Empathy Engine leaves the lab, it becomes many things at once: breakthrough, sensation, temptation, and threat. It offers healing, but also exposure. It promises connection, but it invites hunger, addiction, and the possibility that the most private part of human life might become the next thing to be copied, sold, and consumed.

What It Feels Like to Be You is a literary hard-science-fiction novel about loneliness, mercy, and the dangerous beauty of being known. It asks whether understanding another person can save us without turning the human interior into a marketplace.

At its heart, this is not a novel about a machine.

It is a novel about the distances between people, and what it costs to cross them.
