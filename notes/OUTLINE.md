# OUTLINE.md

**Status:** Working 18-chapter outline  
**Purpose:** Drafting scaffold for the novel  
**Canon source:** If this outline ever conflicts with AGENTS.md, AGENTS.md wins.

## Structural Overview

- **Act I (Chapters 1-6):** The breakthrough leaves the lab
- **Act II (Chapters 7-14):** Promise, temptation, addiction, and exposure
- **Act III (Chapters 15-18):** Truth, relinquishment, and belonging

This outline is meant to preserve the core emotional logic already established:
- the novel begins in pride and dread
- the machine briefly appears miraculous
- its dangers emerge through addiction, privacy breach, and commercialization
- Matt's deepest movement is from guilt and intensity toward responsibility, love, and acceptance
- the ending lands not in spectacle, but in chosen family

## Act I — The Breakthrough Leaves the Lab

### Chapter 1 — The Tip
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Open the novel with success already achieved and danger already present.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt, Piyush, and Shelby celebrate at a high-end Los Angeles steakhouse after the first successful human trial earlier that day.
	- Their waiter is Marcus, working the room with the strained composure of someone trying hard to hold himself together.
	- Through Matt's thoughts and the dinner conversation, the reader learns what happened in the trial: a therapist was able to feel a bipolar patient's emotional state well enough to gain treatment insight.
	- The success is morally complicated from the start because the patient, despite consenting, later feels that their inner world has been violated.
	- Matt feels both pride and dread. He knows they have done something extraordinary, but he also senses that they have opened something they may not be able to close.
	- Beneath the professional dread is the private thought he almost never names: if something like this had existed twenty years ago, he might have understood Tifa and not lost her.
	- Shelby speaks the language of rollout, scale, and inevitability long before Matt is ready.
	- At the end of dinner, Shelby leaves an absurdly generous tip and remarks, in essence, that people will pay far more than that to feel understood.
- **Ending turn:** Matt realizes The Empathy Engine will not stay contained.

### Chapter 2 — Breakfast
- **POV:** Piyush
- **Core function:** Contrast Matt's loneliness with Piyush's rooted family life and show how quickly the outside world invades it.
- **Major beats:**
	- The next morning, Piyush is at home making breakfast.
	- Mira and Nikhil move through the kitchen; Nikhil sings his song about tying your shoe.
	- The domestic rhythm is warm, specific, and ordinary — exactly the kind of life Matt envies.
	- Even in the middle of that warmth, Piyush has a disloyal flicker of envy for Matt's wider life: the travel, the stories, the unrooted freedom that now feels permanently closed to him.
	- Piyush's private fear surfaces: success may devour the family life it was supposed to improve.
	- His phone begins exploding with messages.
	- Lina turns on a screen and sees Shelby already on a morning show, publicly unveiling The Empathy Engine.
- **Ending turn:** Piyush understands that ordinary life is already being swallowed by the machine's public future.

### Chapter 3 — Happy Birthday
- **POV:** Shelby
- **Core function:** Show that Shelby is not a cartoon villain; she is sincere, ambitious, wounded, and already committed to forcing the future.
- **Major beats:**
	- Shelby sits in a makeup chair before the morning show.
	- On her phone is a text from her estranged father: “Happy Birthday.”
	- She feels rage, then swallows it.
	- She privately rationalizes what she is about to do: scale will help people, heal people, and change history — and yes, it will also make her powerful.
	- On air, she names The Empathy Engine and promises a clinical rollout within a year.
	- She frames it as a humanitarian breakthrough for therapy, diplomacy, and repairing damaged relationships.
- **Ending turn:** Shelby makes a public promise Matt and Piyush can no longer walk back.

### Chapter 4 — I Want This
- **POV:** Marcus
- **Core function:** Seed the addiction tragedy early through an ordinary person who sees in the machine a promise of relief.
- **Major beats:**
	- Before the restaurant opens, Marcus watches Shelby on television.
	- He is trying to stay sober from ketamine.
	- His life is emotionally constricted: he is closeted, lonely, and still shaped by a poor, broken home and a homophobic father whose idea of masculinity he still tries to perform.
	- Watching the segment, he has a simple, vulnerable response: “I want this.”
	- Later, he receives an anonymous email with the subject line: “What It Feels Like To Be You.”
	- The email contains a link to a small illicit cache of recorded emotional sessions, apparently stolen from internal test data.
	- Marcus clicks the first recording: the feeling of having sex, not only as pleasure but as intimacy and release.
- **Ending turn:** Marcus discovers the first black-market portal into borrowed feeling.

### Chapter 5 — Launch
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Move from publicity to concrete loss of control.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt rides alone in a Waymo to the lab, already overwhelmed by the morning's betrayal.
	- Piyush calls and tells him Shelby has arranged a private demonstration for two diplomats — without clearing it with either of them.
	- At the lab, lingering ethical fallout from the first therapy trial becomes impossible to ignore: consent did not prepare the patient for how exposed they would feel.
	- Matt and Piyush confront Shelby.
	- Shelby uses money, influence, and moral rhetoric to frame resistance as cowardice.
	- Matt and Piyush reluctantly agree to a tightly controlled session rather than lose all say over how it happens.
- **Ending turn:** The Indian and Pakistani delegations are already on their way.

### Chapter 6 — I Thought Only We Buried Children
- **POV:** Split between Farah and Arjun
- **Core function:** Deliver the novel's first undeniable miracle and end Act I on irreversible expansion.
- **Major beats:**
	- In separate secure prep rooms, Farah looks at a photo of her son and Arjun removes his wedding ring before the session.
	- Each enters determined to make the other side feel the pain their nation has caused.
	- The onset of The Empathy Engine feels like déjà vu.
	- Instead of hatred, each first receives the other's grief.
	- Then each receives the other's compassion for that grief.
	- The emotional logic of the conflict breaks open around the shared fact that both have buried children.
	- One of them says: “I thought only we buried children.”
	- They emerge before cameras shaken and altered, not triumphant.
	- In full public view, they embrace.
	- Shelby immediately pushes the moment into the world as proof that the machine can change history.
- **Ending turn:** Act I ends with the machine transformed from a guarded invention into a global geopolitical event.

## Act II — Promise, Temptation, Addiction, and Exposure

### Chapter 7 — You Look Good for 44
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Show fame arriving just as Matt's private past returns.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt is buried under interviews, invitations, policy interest, and public projection.
	- He feels the machine becoming symbol, product, hope, threat, and myth all at once.
	- Among the flood is a note from Rachel, a film director and one of the few people in Los Angeles who knew him before this version of him existed, telling him to call when he can.
	- In the middle of this flood, he gets a message from Tifa: “You look good for 44.”
	- His first response is disbelief.
	- He writes back: “I spend a lot of time in the jimjilbangs.”
	- The exchange is light, but it destabilizes him more than the media storm.
- **Ending turn:** As the public story grows louder, the most private story of Matt's life quietly returns.

### Chapter 8 — Koreatown
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Reintroduce Tifa with restraint and gentleness rather than melodrama.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt and Tifa meet in a quiet Korean café in Koreatown.
	- Both expect awkwardness; instead, the conversation is surprisingly easy.
	- Tifa tells Matt she is proud of him.
	- Matt tells Tifa she inspired him.
	- We learn she became a family physician, has a stable marriage, and is raising three children.
	- Shu knows about Matt and Tifa's history.
	- The chapter does not resolve the past. It simply makes it possible to imagine that truth might be bearable.
- **Ending turn:** Matt leaves the café with the first real sense that closure may exist.

### Chapter 9 — The Archive
- **POV:** Marcus
- **Core function:** Show emotional dependency forming in private before the world names it as danger.
- **Major beats:**
	- Marcus returns to the archive and keeps returning.
	- The recordings escalate from sex to joy, safety, wonder, and emotional fullness.
	- The feeling that grips him most is the one he never truly had: waking on Christmas morning as a child and seeing gifts under the tree.
	- The recordings give him innocence, safety, and belonging by proxy.
	- He tells himself after nearly every session that it will be the last one.
	- His sobriety from ketamine begins morphing into a different addiction.
	- HumansRHumans continues feeding the small black-market cache in drips from the shadows, enough to keep dependence alive without making the breach public.
- **Ending turn:** Marcus is no longer experimenting — he is using.

### Chapter 10 — Clinic Days
- **POV:** Piyush
- **Core function:** Show the machine in controlled use while making clear that even “responsible access” reshapes ordinary life.
- **Major beats:**
	- Limited clinic use begins under Shelby's pressure, still framed as careful and therapeutic.
	- Piyush is buried in protocols, oversight, logistics, and public expectation.
	- He feels his research life mutating into an industry.
	- Shelby still talks in rollout language, but family-repair cases unsettle her more than she lets on; each one seems to brush against something raw in her own life.
	- Rachel reaches out to Matt, asking to use the machine to accelerate trauma therapy.
	- In a private after-hours Qualia clinic room, Lina and Piyush use the machine in a careful, bounded session.
	- Lina experiences how much Piyush looks up to and respects Matt.
	- Her sense of Matt begins to shift from coworker-adjacent outsider toward someone deeper in the family orbit.
- **Ending turn:** Even its gentlest uses prove that The Empathy Engine changes relationships in ways no one fully controls.

### Chapter 11 — Rachel
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Give the technology its most emotionally credible therapeutic use before showing how vulnerable that goodness is.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt helps Rachel enter a therapy session using the machine.
	- Rachel wants faster access to trauma she has spent years circling.
	- The session opens buried childhood sexual abuse with terrible clarity.
	- The work is painful, but it seems real, intimate, and potentially healing.
	- For a brief moment, Matt believes restricted therapeutic use may justify everything.
	- Unknown to the participants, the session data is compromised.
- **Ending turn:** The promise of healing is real — and therefore what happens next will hurt far more.

### Chapter 12 — Access Denied
- **POV:** Marcus
- **Core function:** Deliver the midpoint tragedy and prove the machine can become the ultimate drug.
- **Major beats:**
	- Marcus's dependency deepens until the archive matters more than work, contact, or recovery.
	- He lives between cravings for borrowed feeling and the flatness of his own unborrowed life.
	- Then, abruptly, his access is cut off.
	- Whether by design or tactical cruelty, the archive deactivates his account after dependence is already in place.
	- He makes one brief, genuine attempt to save himself — reaching toward a meeting, a sponsor, any ordinary human voice — but the return to unborrowed life feels intolerably thin.
	- Marcus cannot tolerate the return to himself.
	- He dies by suicide.
- **Ending turn:** The midpoint confirms that recorded emotion can function as a lethal narcotic.

### Chapter 13 — The Human Soul for Sale
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Take Matt to his lowest moral and emotional point.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt learns that the dead user was Marcus, the waiter from the opening dinner.
	- Rachel's therapy breakdown is leaked by HumansRHumans and turns instantly into scandal.
	- Rachel tries to fight back publicly; trolls and haters mob her.
	- The world now understands publicly and at scale that emotional sessions can be captured, replayed, copied, and exploited.
	- Even Shelby, for once, seems shaken rather than merely strategic, though she still has not surrendered her instinct to control the fallout.
	- Matt sees that he has not simply built a bridge between people; he may have helped build a market in stolen feeling.
	- Congress opens an inquiry and summons him to testify.
- **Ending turn:** Matt believes he has helped commercialize the human soul.

### Chapter 14 — Shoe-Tying Song
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Reorient Matt's understanding of what life and empathy are for.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt goes to Piyush's house near collapse.
	- Nikhil plays his song about tying your shoe.
	- Later, in a private after-hours Qualia session, Piyush and Matt use the machine together.
	- Matt feels Piyush's pride in his child.
	- He realizes he has spent years mistaking intensity for depth.
	- He sees that the truest greatness may be ordinary presence, witness, and care.
	- Lina's warmth toward him shifts further; the family begins, quietly, to make room for him.
- **Ending turn:** Matt recovers enough moral clarity to choose truth over self-protection.

## Act III — Truth, Relinquishment, and Belonging

### Chapter 15 — Love
- **POV:** Split between Matt and Tifa
- **Core function:** Resolve the novel's deepest emotional question before the public climax.
- **Major beats:**
	- Before Congress, Matt and Tifa agree to one private session in The Empathy Engine.
	- The first transmitted emotion is love.
	- Matt finally understands that Tifa herself was not ready for marriage then; she wanted to go back to school and could not yet admit that even to herself.
	- Tifa finally understands that Matt was not merely selfish; he was frightened, trapped in a false script, and unable to articulate what he was fleeing.
	- Their breakup is redefined from simple betrayal into a tragedy of mutual misunderstanding.
	- They do not reunite romantically.
	- They release one another with grief, gratitude, and continued care.
- **Ending turn:** Matt carries love, grief, clarity, and quiet steadiness into Congress.

### Chapter 16 — Afraid
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Deliver the moral climax: stewardship over legacy.
- **Major beats:**
	- Matt appears before Congress.
	- He is honest about the machine's therapeutic promise, diplomatic power, addictive danger, false-consent problem, and privacy catastrophe.
	- He does not defend the commercial future of his invention.
	- The hardest truth he speaks is simple: he is afraid of the machine.
	- He acknowledges that HumansRHumans exposed real dangers, even though their methods were cruel and destructive.
	- His testimony sacrifices scale, prestige, and much of what Qualia Labs was built to become.
	- Congress classifies The Empathy Engine as a dangerous, tightly controlled intervention limited to therapeutic, diplomatic, and tightly controlled clinical use.
- **Ending turn:** The consumer future dies; ethical survival remains possible.

### Chapter 17 — Shame
- **POV:** Shelby
- **Core function:** Complete Shelby's arc through costly relinquishment rather than abstract remorse.
- **Major beats:**
	- After Congress, Shelby goes to her father.
	- In the session, she first feels his shame.
	- He first feels her fear.
	- He tries to rip away from the experience, then stays.
	- For the first time, he fully recognizes what he made home feel like for her.
	- He cries and apologizes.
	- Shelby reconciles with him, not because the past is erased, but because the truth is finally shared.
	- Changed by this, Shelby gives up ownership and control so The Empathy Engine can never become a mass-market product.
- **Ending turn:** Shelby relinquishes empire in favor of limits.

### Chapter 18 — Uncle Matt
- **POV:** Matt
- **Core function:** Land the novel in belonging rather than triumph.
- **Major beats:**
	- Some time has passed.
	- The Empathy Engine survives only inside a tightly restricted therapeutic, diplomatic, and clinical framework.
	- Matt has accepted a quieter, more ethical place in relation to it.
	- At Griffith Park, he stands with Piyush watching Mira and Nikhil play.
	- Lina treats him as family.
	- Matt has not received wish fulfillment, but he has received something truer: self-forgiveness, purpose, and a place among people he loves.
- **Final image:** not spectacle, not innovation, not history — just belonging.

## Drafting Notes

- Keep the novel restrained even in high-emotion scenes.
- The machine should remain bounded: raw emotion, not thoughts or memories.
- The miracle of the diplomats and the tragedy of Marcus should feel equally real.
- Tifa's return should heal Matt without becoming romantic endgame.
- The final movement should feel earned, peaceful, and unmistakably uplifting.
